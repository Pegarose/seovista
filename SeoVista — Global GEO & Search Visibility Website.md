# SeoVista — Global GEO & Search Visibility Website PRD v1.0

<aside>
🎯

**Product thesis:** SeoVista helps ambitious brands become **found, understood and cited** across traditional search and AI-generated answers. Default language is English; GEO is the lead category, while SEO, digital authority and publisher partnerships support it.

</aside>

## 1. Strategic decision

SeoVista will launch as a **global, premium search visibility company**, not as a Turkish backlink marketplace and not as a clone of Semrush or Semust.

- **Primary market:** Global companies, international marketing teams and agencies.
- **Default language:** English at `/`.
- **Secondary language:** Turkish may launch later under `/tr/`; it must not delay the English launch.
- **Primary conversion:** `Get a GEO Audit`.
- **Secondary conversion:** Use a free visibility tool and leave qualified contact information.
- **Brand relationship:** `SeoVista — A GMedya Group company` in the footer and About page; GMedya should not dominate the hero.
- **Business model at launch:** Expert-led services + assessments. Do not present SeoVista as a mature SaaS platform until the tools and recurring product genuinely exist.

The advantage is not “more SEO tools.” It is connecting **Owned Content + Technical Entity Signals + Earned Media Authority** in one operating model.

## 7. Free tools and lead-generation system

The tools must provide immediate value first and ask for contact information at a natural point. Do not lock every result behind a form.

### MVP 1 — GEO Readiness Checker

**Inputs:** domain, brand name, primary market, work email.

**Checks that can be delivered credibly:**

- robots.txt access for major search and AI crawlers
- sitemap presence
- canonical and indexability signals
- organization/person/article schema presence
- entity consistency indicators
- author and editorial-policy signals
- llms.txt presence as an informational check—not a ranking factor claim
- answer-first content structure indicators
- first-party evidence and citation patterns

**Output:** score by Access, Understanding, Evidence and Authority + three prioritized actions.

**Lead capture:** Show a useful summary instantly. Email the detailed PDF/report after explicit consent.

### MVP 2 — AI Crawler Access Checker

URL/domain input → status for major crawler directives, conflicting rules and recommended fixes. No email required for basic output; email optional for full technical report.

### MVP 3 — Schema & Entity Checker

Detect schema types, missing Organization fields, `sameAs` consistency and author/entity gaps. Provide copyable recommendations.

### MVP 4 — SERP Preview

Title/meta preview with pixel guidance, truncation warning and shareable result. This is an acquisition utility, not a core differentiator.

### Phase 2 tools

- AI Visibility Benchmark — requires a legitimate prompt-testing/data methodology
- Citation Gap Explorer
- Brand Entity Consistency Scanner
- Answer Readiness Grader
- International hreflang checker

### Lead form fields

Keep the first form short:

- Work email
- Website
- Company
- Primary market
- Primary challenge: AI visibility / SEO / content / digital authority / agency partnership

Ask budget, team size and phone only after initial value is delivered or on the consultation form.

### Consent and data rules

- Clear purpose statement next to form
- Separate marketing consent; never pre-checked
- Privacy link at collection point
- Retention policy for audit data
- Rate limiting, CAPTCHA and abuse protection
- Do not expose audit results publicly without consent

## 8. Visual direction — global premium

### Design concept

**Editorial Intelligence Lab:** premium consultancy credibility combined with a precise data product. The site must feel calmer and more authored than typical neon SaaS templates.

### Palette

- `Ink` — `#0A1017`
- `Graphite` — `#121B24`
- `Mineral` — `#F3F1EB`
- `Paper` — `#FCFBF7`
- `Signal Green` — `#66E3B4`
- `Spectral Blue` — `#6C8CFF`
- `Muted` — `#697684`
- `Border Dark` — `rgba(255,255,255,.12)`
- `Border Light` — `#D8DDD9`

Signal Green and Spectral Blue are for data states, focus and small gradients—not decorative colored words in every headline. Do not imitate Semrush orange/purple or Semust’s component styling.

### Typography

- **Display:** Geist Sans or Manrope, 600–700
- **Body/UI:** Inter or Geist Sans, 400–600
- **Editorial accent:** optional Instrument Serif Italic, maximum twice per page
- Large headlines use controlled line lengths; no exaggerated 100px type on mobile.

### Layout and interaction

- 1320px max container; generous whitespace
- Alternating mineral editorial surfaces and deep-ink data sections
- Thin graph lines, evidence cards and citation maps instead of stock illustrations
- Motion limited to opacity/transform; 200/350/600ms; reduced-motion support
- Interactive hero can show a question flowing through sources into an attributed answer
- Real tool states, not decorative fake dashboards
- No stock 3D characters, floating glass cards, random blobs, infinite marquees or crypto aesthetics

### Visual signature

A **Citation Graph** motif: sources, entities and answer nodes connected by fine lines. It should appear in the hero, section dividers, tool results and reports, becoming a recognizable SeoVista asset.

## 10. Technical architecture — binding

### Core stack

- **Web:** Next.js App Router + React Server Components + TypeScript strict mode
- **UI:** Tailwind CSS v4 using project-level `@theme` design tokens
- **CMS:** GMedya's proprietary **NextG CMS**; Payload CMS must not be installed
- **Primary database:** PostgreSQL
- **Background processing:** Redis + BullMQ in a separate worker service
- **Files and generated reports:** S3-compatible object storage or Cloudflare R2
- **Edge, DNS, WAF and caching:** Cloudflare
- **Repository:** pnpm monorepo
- **Testing:** Vitest, Playwright, Lighthouse CI and axe

### Repository structure

```
apps/
  web/                   Next.js marketing site, tools and result views
  nextg/                 NextG CMS application/integration
  worker/                Audits, scheduled jobs and report generation

packages/
  ui/                    SeoVista design system
  seo-core/              Metadata, canonicals, robots and sitemap logic
  schema/                Central JSON-LD graph builders
  content-models/        Shared NextG content contracts
  audit-core/            Framework-independent crawl and audit checks
  open-seo-adapter/      Selected MIT-licensed OpenSEO adaptations
  dataforseo/            DataForSEO client, caching and cost controls
  geo-engine/            SeoVista-owned GEO scoring and analysis
  reports/               Email and PDF report generation
  analytics/             Typed analytics events
```

### Rendering rules

- Public editorial and commercial content must be present in the first HTML response.
- Use Server Components by default; never mark an entire route tree as `use client`.
- Use ISR for stable public pages and insights; SSR only where request-time data is necessary.
- Client Components are limited to forms, filters, charts and interactive tool states.
- The core meaning of a page must remain accessible when client JavaScript fails.

### NextG CMS content model

NextG must provide structured collections for Pages, Services, Tools, Articles, Authors, Organizations, Case Studies, Research Reports, Definitions, FAQs, Sources, Redirects, Locales and Audit Leads.

Editorial content must support:

- short answer/summary
- author and expert reviewer
- publish and modified dates
- citations and primary sources
- main and related entities
- original data/research flags
- FAQs
- canonical URL
- locale and translated equivalents
- social image and indexation controls

JSON-LD must be generated from these structured fields—not inferred from arbitrary rich text.

### OpenSEO reuse policy

[OpenSEO](https://github.com/every-app/open-seo) is an **engine donor**, not the application foundation and not the SeoVista user interface.

Approved candidates for adaptation:

1. DataForSEO request adapters, normalization and cost references
2. technical site-audit checks and the `badseo` test fixtures
3. Google Search Console OAuth/data flow
4. MCP server patterns and selected agent skills
5. rank, keyword, backlink and competitor workflow logic where relevant

Do not adopt:

- Vite/TanStack application shell
- DaisyUI interface
- OpenSEO branding or page layouts
- Cloudflare-specific D1/Durable Objects architecture as SeoVista's core
- marketing copy or unsupported AI Visibility claims

All reused code must be reviewed, refactored into framework-independent packages and pinned to a specific upstream commit. Preserve MIT copyright/license notices in `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`. Never auto-merge upstream changes.

### SeoVista-owned GEO layer

The following are proprietary SeoVista components and must not be represented as delivered by OpenSEO:

- GEO Readiness scoring methodology
- entity and schema analysis model
- Citation Graph visualization and data contract
- AI citation extraction and normalization
- prompt-set, market and model measurement methodology
- Share of Model, citation-rate and sentiment calculations
- GEO recommendations and report language

An “AI Visibility” label in an upstream repository is not evidence that these capabilities are accurate or production-ready.

### Audit execution model

1. Next.js validates the request and creates an audit record.
2. A job is added to Redis/BullMQ.
3. The worker fetches only publicly accessible URLs and applies SSRF protections.
4. Results are normalized and stored in PostgreSQL.
5. A useful summary is shown immediately when ready.
6. Detailed reports are generated and emailed only with explicit consent.
7. Private audit/result routes remain authenticated or token-protected, `noindex` and outside all sitemaps.

API credentials, especially DataForSEO, Google OAuth and model-provider keys, must remain server-side. Apply per-IP/account rate limits, request quotas, caching, timeouts, cost ceilings and abuse monitoring.

### SEO and JSON-LD requirements

- `trailingSlash: true`
- self-referencing canonicals
- lowercase permanent slugs and single-hop 301 redirects
- EN at root; future TR under `/tr/`
- hreflang only between true translated equivalents
- XML sitemaps generated from canonical, indexable, HTTP 200 records
- centralized server-rendered `@graph` JSON-LD with stable `@id` values
- Organization + WebSite on the site entity layer
- Service + BreadcrumbList on service pages
- Article/BlogPosting + Person + BreadcrumbList on editorial pages
- WebApplication/SoftwareApplication only for real, functioning tools
- FAQPage only when the same questions and answers are visibly present and eligible
- SeoVista Organization connects to GMedya through `parentOrganization`
- no fabricated AggregateRating, reviews, customers, datasets or performance claims

### GEO discoverability requirements

- intentional robots policy for search and approved AI crawlers
- `llms.txt` as an informational discovery aid, never described as a ranking factor
- semantic HTML and answer-first information blocks
- visible authorship, reviewer, source, methodology and modified-date signals
- entity-consistent About, author and organization pages
- citation-ready definitions, tables, facts and original research
- RSS/Atom feed for insights
- private/admin routes must never be opened to crawlers

### Performance and accessibility gates

- LCP ≤2.5s, target ≤1.8s
- INP ≤200ms
- CLS ≤0.1, target ≤0.05
- TTFB ≤800ms, target ≤500ms
- mobile Lighthouse Performance ≥90
- Lighthouse SEO = 100 on representative templates
- Accessibility ≥95 and WCAG 2.2 AA
- keyboard-complete forms and tools

### Deployment quality gate

CI must fail for critical errors in:

- TypeScript strict checking and linting
- unit and Playwright smoke tests
- broken internal links
- missing/duplicate canonical, title or description
- invalid JSON-LD
- sitemap URLs returning non-200, redirect or `noindex`
- robots/noindex conflicts
- Lighthouse budget regression
- axe accessibility violations
- missing image dimensions/alt policy violations

Typed analytics events: `tool_start`, `tool_complete`, `report_request`, `audit_request`, `qualified_lead`, `audit_error`, `api_cost_recorded`.

## 12. Acceptance criteria

The launch is accepted only if:

1. A visitor understands within five seconds that SeoVista is about GEO and search visibility.
2. The website looks like an international specialist company—not a Turkish backlink seller or generic AI template.
3. Every live feature works; no “coming soon” cards appear in primary navigation.
4. At least one useful free tool produces a real result.
5. Lead capture is consent-compliant and connected to an owned workflow.
6. No unsupported performance, customer, dataset or guarantee claims exist.
7. Sponsored-content infrastructure remains operational without making BasınOdam and SeoVista appear to be duplicate brands.
8. [Backlinkler.com](http://Backlinkler.com) and [BacklinkWire.com](http://BacklinkWire.com) are not presented as public sub-brands at launch.
9. GMedya can link to a complete, credible SeoVista destination.
10. Mobile, accessibility, performance and structured-data checks pass before launch.
